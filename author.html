Chief Stephen Ayodele Fatiregun

Biography

Stephen Ayodele Fatiregun was born in Lagos on Saturday 29th July 1939. He is a member of the Pioneer set (1953/58) of Ansar-Ud-Deen College, Isolo, where he sat and passed the Yoruba Language subject with distinction (‘A’ class) in the West African School Certificate Examination (WASCE) held in December 1958. Fatiregun is a Graduate of Business Administration, Harvard University, where he successfully completed the Harvard International Senior Managers Program in 1981. He is a Fellow of the Chartered Association of Certified Accountants in London and an Associate Member of the Institute of Chartered Accountants in Nigeria (ICAN). He is also a Graduate of Natural Medicine at the Nigerian College of Natural Medicine, Victoria Island, Lagos and a Graduate (with distinction) of Herbal Medicine, University of Lagos (UNILAG)

Ayo was the President of Ijebu-Jesha Social Club for seven years (1992-1999). In recognition of his contributions to the growth and development of his ancestral home, he was conferred with the Chieftaincy title of ‘OBALUA OF EGBOROLAND’ in Ijebu-Jesa, Osun State.

He is a tireless researcher of African Traditional beliefs and culture – a passionate lover of Yoruba Language and Literature.


Career

  • Accountant, Federal Ministry of Finance (1970)
  • Chief Accountant and later, Acting General Manager, Nigerian Office Stationery Supply Stores Limited (1970-1972)
  • Chief Accountant, Hagemeyer (Nigeria) Limited (1972-1975)
  • Financial Controller, Hagemeyer (Nigeria) Limited (1975-1977)
  • Executive Director, Hagemeyer (Nigeria) Limited (1977-1980)
  • Deputy Managing Director, Hagemeyer (Nigeria) Limited (1980-1982)
  • First Nigerian Managing Director, Hagemeyer (Nigeria) Limited (1982-1993)
  • Executive Vice-Chairman, Hagemeyer (Nigeria) Limited (1993-1994)
  • Chairman, Hagemeyer (Nigeria) Limited (now DN Meyer Plc.) (1994-1999)
  • Director, Dunlop (Nigeria) Plc. (1994-1999)
